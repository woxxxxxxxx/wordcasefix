What to Look for in Payroll Software for Small Teams

Running payroll incorrectly is expensive — IRS penalties for payroll tax errors averaged $845 per incident in 2025. For small teams, the key requirements are: automated tax calculation and filing (federal + state), direct deposit, W-2/1099 generation at year-end, and compliance with state-specific requirements like paid leave laws. Bonus features: employee self-service portal, benefits administration, and HR tools.

We tested 7 payroll platforms by running actual payroll for test employees in multiple states, filing test returns, and evaluating every part of the onboarding experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

"Full-service payroll" means the software handles tax filing and payments on your behalf — it automatically calculates federal, state, and local payroll taxes, files the returns, and pays the taxes from your account. The alternative is "self-service payroll" where you calculate taxes yourself and file manually. For most small businesses, full-service payroll is worth every penny — payroll tax errors are expensive, and the time savings are significant. All platforms in this article offer full-service payroll.

For a team of 5 employees: Homebase: ~$40/mo (base + $4 × 5). Gusto Simple: $70/mo ($40 base + $6 × 5). OnPay: $70/mo ($40 + $6 × 5). QuickBooks Payroll Core: $80/mo ($50 + $6 × 5). ADP/Paychex: typically $100+/mo (custom pricing). Homebase is the most affordable full-service option for very small teams. Gusto and OnPay are mid-tier. QuickBooks, ADP, and Paychex are premium options.

Yes — most modern payroll platforms support both employees (W-2) and contractors (1099). Gusto, OnPay, Rippling, and ADP all allow you to pay contractors with direct deposit, collect W-9 information digitally, and generate 1099-NEC forms at year-end. This is important: paying contractors through payroll software keeps your records clean and makes 1099 filing automatic at year-end.

With full-service payroll, the software provider bears responsibility for tax filing errors caused by their calculations. Gusto, OnPay, and QuickBooks Payroll all offer error protection guarantees — if their calculation is wrong, they'll pay the penalties. This is a major advantage of full-service over self-service payroll. Keep your employee information current (hours, deductions, withholding allowances) and the platforms will handle the rest accurately.
