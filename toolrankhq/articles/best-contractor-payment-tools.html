The Real Cost of Paying Contractors Wrong

Paying contractors seems simple — just send them money, right? But the compliance requirements are significant: you need to collect W-9 forms from US contractors, send 1099-NEC forms for anyone paid over $600/year, comply with international withholding requirements for foreign contractors, and maintain records proving the contractor relationship isn't actually employment.

The right tool handles all of this automatically. The wrong approach — sending PayPal payments with no documentation — leaves you exposed to IRS penalties, potential back-tax liability, and misclassification claims. We tested 6 tools on compliance completeness, payment speed, fees, and ease of use.

Frequently Asked Questions

You must issue a 1099-NEC to any US individual contractor paid $600 or more during the tax year. This includes sole proprietors and single-member LLCs. You don't issue 1099s to corporations (C-corps or S-corps) unless paying for legal or medical services. For foreign contractors, you may need to withhold taxes and file Form 1042-S. All platforms on this list automate these thresholds and generate the appropriate forms.

Misclassification is one of the most expensive payroll mistakes. If the IRS determines someone you've been paying as a contractor should have been classified as an employee, you become liable for: back payroll taxes (employer share of FICA — 7.65%), potential penalties (up to 100% of unpaid taxes in egregious cases), back benefits if applicable, and potential state penalties which can be even steeper than federal. Gusto and Deel both include contractor vs. employee classification guidance to help avoid these costly errors.

Most platforms charge a spread on currency conversion. Deel and Wise are the most transparent about exact conversion rates and fees.

For US-based contractors paid $600 or more in a calendar year, you need to file a 1099-NEC form with the IRS and send a copy to the contractor by January 31. Before paying a contractor, collect a completed W-9 form from them — this captures their Tax ID (SSN or EIN) needed for 1099 filing. Gusto, Deel, and Remote all automate the W-9 collection and 1099 generation process, significantly reducing your end-of-year tax preparation burden. Non-US contractors typically receive Form 1042-S instead if any US-source income is involved.
