What Are Deel, Remote, and Rippling For?

These three platforms exist to solve the increasingly common problem of paying people in other countries. Whether you're a US company paying overseas contractors, or hiring full-time employees in other countries without establishing a local entity, these platforms handle the legal, tax, and payment complexity.

Two key concepts: Contractor payments (you pay freelancers/contractors internationally via the platform) and Employer of Record (EOR) (the platform becomes the legal employer in a foreign country so you can hire full-time employees there without setting up a local company).

Frequently Asked Questions

Contractor payment: you pay an independent contractor in another country through a platform that handles currency conversion and local tax documentation. The worker is self-employed and not your legal employee. EOR (Employer of Record): the platform becomes the legal employer in a country, allowing you to hire someone as a full-time employee with local benefits and protections — without you needing to register a company in that country. EOR is appropriate for long-term, full-time workers. Contractors should only be used for genuinely independent work.

Remote is generally considered safer for EOR because it operates its own legal entities in 80+ countries rather than relying on third-party partners in all markets. When Remote is your EOR, it's directly responsible for compliance. Deel uses a mix of owned entities and third-party partners depending on the country. For contractor payments, both platforms are equally compliant — the compliance risk in contractor scenarios is misclassification, which both platforms help prevent through their contract templates.

Deel typically processes payments within 2–5 business days depending on the destination country and payment method. Bank transfers are faster and cheaper than local payroll methods. Remote's contractor payments (Contractor Management, $29/mo) process in 2–3 business days. Rippling's global payroll timing depends on the country. For same-week payments, Deel has the edge with its instant payment option in select countries via Deel Card.
