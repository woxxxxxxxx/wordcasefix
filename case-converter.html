What Is a Case Converter — and When Do You Need One?

A case converter transforms text between five standard case styles: UPPERCASE (all capitals), lowercase (all small letters), Title Case (major words capitalized), Sentence case (first letter of each sentence), and aLtErNaTiNg (every other character flips). What used to require manual editing or a text editor macro now takes a single click.

Writers and bloggers use it to format headlines consistently — Title Case for post titles, Sentence case for subheadings — without memorizing capitalization rules for every preposition and conjunction. SEO professionals use it to normalize metadata: a page title in UPPERCASE copied from a spreadsheet needs to become Title Case before it goes into a CMS. Developers use it to reformat variable names, code comments, and documentation that arrives in the wrong case from third-party sources.

Students benefit from Sentence case conversion when pasting text from PDFs or slides that arrive in ALL CAPS — a common artifact of copied PowerPoint content. Social media managers use alternating case for stylistic effect in posts and memes. The tool handles inputs of any length instantly, and everything runs in your browser, so your text is never uploaded anywhere.

The five conversions this tool supports cover 99% of real-world needs. Bookmark it alongside your other everyday writing tools, and you'll never manually fix capitalization again.
