Pig Latin Rules
  1. Words beginning with a vowel (a, e, i, o, u): add "way" to the end. Example: apple → appleway
  2. Words beginning with consonants: move all consonants before the first vowel to the end, then add "ay". Example: hello → ellohay
  3. Capitalization is preserved on the new first letter.
  4. Punctuation is kept in place.

About the Pig Latin Converter

Pig Latin is a fun word game and language game where English words are altered by moving consonant clusters from the beginning of the word to the end and adding a vowel suffix. Words starting with vowels simply get "way" appended. It originated as playground slang in English-speaking countries and is popular with children and in casual conversation. This free Pig Latin converter handles all standard rules including consonant cluster movement, capitalization preservation, and punctuation handling.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pig Latin?

Pig Latin is an English language game where words are altered according to a simple rule set: consonants are moved to the end and "ay" is added, or for vowel-starting words, "way" is appended.

Example: how is "hello" converted?

"hello" starts with consonants "h", so move "h" to the end and add "ay": hello → ello + h + ay = ellohay.
