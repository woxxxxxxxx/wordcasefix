Questions to ask before buying

  1. Do I have employees, owners, officers, or contractors who affect workers compensation obligations?
  2. Do I use vehicles for business, deliveries, jobsites, or client visits?
  3. Do clients, landlords, vendors, or licensing bodies require proof of insurance?
  4. Do I own tools, inventory, tenant improvements, or equipment that should be protected?

Suggested official resources

Check your state insurance department, workers compensation agency, secretary of state, licensing board, and local business resources before making compliance decisions. Requirements and agency names vary by state.

Quote preparation for New York businesses

Prepare a short business description, NAICS or industry category if known, owner/officer information, employee count, payroll estimate, annual revenue, business property values, vehicle details, lease requirements, customer contract wording, and any prior insurance claims. If you operate in multiple states, ask how coverage and workers compensation rules apply across locations.

Frequently asked questions

Is workers compensation required in New York?

Yes. New York requires workers comp for nearly all employers, including most one-person corporations and LLCs. The New York State Workers' Compensation Board oversees compliance and penalties can exceed $2,000 per 10-day period.

Does New York require disability benefits insurance?

Yes. New York is one of a few states that requires statutory short-term disability benefits (DBL) and Paid Family Leave coverage for employees, in addition to workers compensation.

What are New York commercial auto liability minimums?

New York requires $25,000/$50,000 bodily injury and $10,000 property damage, plus mandatory $50,000 personal injury protection. Most business contracts require $1M combined single limit.

Is general liability insurance required for New York contractors?

It is not state-mandated, but New York City requires general liability and workers comp for licensed home improvement contractors, and most commercial projects require $1M/$2M limits with additional insured endorsements.

How does NYC affect business insurance compared to upstate New York?

NYC underwriting often carries higher premiums for general liability, commercial auto, and workers comp due to dense population, litigation patterns, and traffic. Industry classifications and location ZIP code are key rating factors.

Where can New York business owners verify requirements?

The New York Department of Financial Services (DFS) regulates insurers, the Workers' Compensation Board oversees workers comp and disability, and NY.gov lists state licensing and insurance requirements.
