  2. Do I use vehicles for business, deliveries, jobsites, or client visits?
  3. Do clients, landlords, vendors, or licensing bodies require proof of insurance?

If you operate in multiple states, ask how coverage and workers compensation rules apply across locations.

Frequently asked questions

Is workers compensation insurance required in Georgia?

Yes. Georgia requires workers comp for any business with three or more employees, including part-time workers and corporate officers (who may exempt themselves under specific conditions).

What are Georgia commercial auto liability minimums?

Georgia requires $25,000 per person, $50,000 per accident bodily injury, and $25,000 property damage. Most business contracts require $1M combined single limit for commercial vehicles.

Does Georgia require general liability insurance?

No state mandate for most businesses, but Georgia general contractors, residential homebuilders, and many professional license holders are required to maintain general liability through the Secretary of State licensing rules.

What insurance do Georgia contractors need to get licensed?

Georgia residential and general contractors must show proof of general liability ($300,000 minimum per occurrence) and workers compensation if applicable. The State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors enforces these rules.

Are there Georgia-specific weather considerations for property insurance?

Yes. Coastal counties carry separate wind and named storm deductibles, and statewide tornado risk affects rating. Flood is excluded from standard property policies and requires a separate NFIP or private flood policy.

Where can Georgia businesses verify insurance requirements?

The Georgia Office of Insurance and Safety Fire Commissioner regulates carriers, the State Board of Workers' Compensation oversees workers comp, and the Secretary of State licensing site lists insurance requirements by profession.
