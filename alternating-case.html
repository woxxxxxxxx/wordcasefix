Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "Mocking SpongeBob" text format?
It's alternating capital and lowercase letters (lIkE tHiS) popularized by a 2017 SpongeBob meme used to mock or sarcastically repeat someone. The format conveys condescension or disbelief in social media replies.
Does alternating case start with uppercase or lowercase?
By default the converter starts with lowercase (hElLo) which matches the original SpongeBob meme. You can toggle which letter starts the pattern; the output then alternates strictly letter by letter, skipping spaces and punctuation.
Will screen readers pronounce alternating case differently?
Most screen readers ignore capitalization and read alternating case as normal words, so accessibility isn't broken. However, search engines may treat the stylized text as a separate token, hurting discoverability of meme-formatted social posts.
