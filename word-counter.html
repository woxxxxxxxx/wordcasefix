Word Counter: Track Every Metric That Matters for Your Writing

A word count is rarely enough. This word counter gives you the full picture in real time: words, characters (with and without spaces), sentences, paragraphs, and an estimated reading time based on average adult reading speed (238 words per minute). Every metric updates as you type — no button to press, no page reload.

Writers use it to hit submission targets — grant applications, academic essays, news articles, and LinkedIn posts all have specific word limits. Bloggers check reading time estimates to gauge whether a post will hold readers to the end or feel too long. Content marketers use the character count to stay within meta description limits (150-160 chars) and tweet length. Students use it to verify their essay meets minimum requirements before submitting.

The sentence and paragraph counts are particularly useful for readability assessment. If your average sentence length is over 25 words, your content scores high on complexity scales like Flesch-Kincaid. Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences) with varied sentence length produce content that reads faster and retains readers. This tool gives you the raw numbers to make informed editing decisions — paste a draft, see where it stands, edit, and measure again until it's right.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Word Counter free to use?

Yes, completely free. Word Counter requires no account, no subscription, and no payment. Use it as often as you like with no limits.

Is there a character or word limit in Word Counter?

No. Word Counter handles text of any length. Paste in as much content as you need and the tool processes it all instantly.

Does Word Counter work in my browser without installation?

Yes. Word Counter runs entirely in your browser using client-side JavaScript. No software, extension, or plugin installation is required.

Is my text kept private when I use Word Counter?

Yes. All text processing in Word Counter happens locally in your browser. Your text is never sent to or stored on any server.

Can I use Word Counter on a mobile device?

Yes. Word Counter is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets as well as desktop computers.
