About Palindrome Checker

The Palindrome Checker determines whether a word or phrase reads the same forwards and backwards. Classic palindromes include "racecar", "level", and "madam". When checking phrases, spaces and punctuation are optionally ignored so that "A man a plan a canal Panama" correctly identifies as a palindrome. Case-insensitive mode treats upper and lowercase letters as equal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a palindrome?

A palindrome is a word, phrase, or sequence that reads the same forward and backward. Examples: "racecar", "level", "madam", "A man a plan a canal Panama".

Why does the phrase check ignore spaces?

A phrase palindrome like "Never odd or even" only works when spaces and punctuation are removed. Ignoring them is the standard way to check phrase palindromes.

Is this tool free?

Yes, completely free with no login required.
