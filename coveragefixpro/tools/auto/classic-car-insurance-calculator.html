How Classic Car Insurance Works

Classic car insurance uses an agreed value model — you and the insurer agree on the car's value upfront, and that full amount is paid in the event of a total loss (no depreciation deducted). The base rate is approximately 1.2% of agreed value annually, much lower than standard insurance:

  • Under 1,000 miles/year: 20% discount (show cars, garage queens)
  • 1,000–5,000 miles/year: Standard classic rate
  • Over 5,000 miles/year: 40% surcharge (frequent use)

Most classic car policies also require secure storage, a primary daily driver vehicle, and may limit pleasure-use only.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as a classic car for insurance purposes?

Most insurers define classic cars as vehicles at least 20–25 years old that are maintained in good to excellent condition and used primarily for shows, exhibitions, or occasional pleasure driving — not daily transportation. Some companies also cover antiques (45+ years old) and exotic/collectible vehicles.

What is agreed value vs actual cash value?

Standard auto insurance pays actual cash value (market value at time of loss, minus depreciation). Classic car insurance pays the agreed value — the full amount you and the insurer agreed upon when the policy was written, with no depreciation deduction. This is critical for collectibles that appreciate over time.

How do insurers verify the agreed value?

Most classic car insurers require a professional appraisal or detailed photographs before insuring for high agreed values. The appraisal should document the vehicle's condition, restoration work, provenance, and current market comparables.

Can I drive my classic car daily?

Most classic car policies prohibit daily commuting use and require you to own a separate primary vehicle. If you want to use your classic more frequently, consider a modified car policy or a standard policy that insures collector cars.

Does classic car insurance cover spare parts?

Many specialty insurers offer spare parts coverage as an add-on, typically covering $500–$2,000 in spare parts stored separately from the vehicle. This is valuable for rare vehicles where parts are difficult to source.
