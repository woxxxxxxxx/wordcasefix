How Good Driver Discounts Work

Insurers reward drivers who maintain a clean record with reduced premiums, typically referred to as a good driver, safe driver, or accident-free discount. To qualify, you generally need zero at-fault accidents and zero moving violations within the past 3 years.

The discount amount grows with your clean driving streak. Drivers with 3+ years clean typically receive 10%, those with 5+ years receive 15%, and those with 7+ years of clean driving often qualify for a 20% discount.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as a violation for this discount?

Moving violations such as speeding tickets, running red lights, and reckless driving typically disqualify you. Non-moving violations (like parking tickets) generally do not affect your eligibility. Not-at-fault accidents usually don't count against you either.

How do I prove I qualify for a good driver discount?

Your insurer will pull your Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) directly. You don't need to provide anything — they check your driving history automatically when you apply or renew your policy.

Does a good driver discount apply automatically?

With most insurers it's applied automatically at renewal if you qualify. However, if you recently switched insurers or believe you qualify but aren't seeing the discount, call your agent and ask specifically about good driver discounts.

Will one ticket remove my good driver discount?

Yes, most insurers will remove the discount if you receive a moving violation or at-fault accident. The impact can last 3–5 years, making the true cost of a single ticket far higher than the fine itself.

Can I stack a good driver discount with other discounts?

Yes. Good driver discounts typically stack with multi-car, bundling, good student, and loyalty discounts. Ask your insurer which discounts can be combined to maximize your savings.
