How This Calculator Works

This tool estimates your car insurance premium using key rating factors that most insurers use. We start with a national base rate and apply multipliers for your age, vehicle year, accident history, and state of residence.

  • Age: Younger drivers (under 25) and seniors (over 65) typically pay more due to higher statistical risk.
  • Vehicle Year: Newer vehicles cost more to insure because parts and repairs are more expensive.
  • Accident History: Each at-fault accident can significantly raise your premiums for 3-5 years.
  • State: Insurance is regulated at the state level, and costs vary widely based on local laws and claim rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of car insurance in the US?

The national average for full coverage car insurance is approximately $1,700 per year, though this varies significantly by state, driver profile, and vehicle type. Liability-only coverage averages around $600 per year.

How can I lower my car insurance premium?

You can lower your premium by maintaining a clean driving record, bundling home and auto insurance, raising your deductible, taking a defensive driving course, and asking about good driver discounts.

Why does my state affect my car insurance rate?

Each state has different minimum coverage requirements, litigation environments, weather risks, and uninsured driver rates. States like Florida and New York tend to have higher rates due to dense traffic and legal environments.

How long do accidents stay on my insurance record?

Most at-fault accidents affect your insurance rates for 3 to 5 years, depending on your insurer and state. Serious accidents may stay on your record longer.

How accurate is this estimate?

This calculator provides an educational estimate based on common industry rating factors. Actual quotes from insurers will vary based on your credit score, vehicle make/model, coverage limits, and many other factors. Always get quotes from multiple insurers for the most accurate pricing.
