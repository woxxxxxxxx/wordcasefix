How Pay-Per-Mile Insurance Works

Pay-per-mile insurance (also called usage-based or mileage-based insurance) charges a fixed monthly base rate plus a per-mile fee tracked via a small device plugged into your car or a smartphone app. The less you drive, the less you pay.

This calculator uses industry-average rates: a $30/month base rate ($360/year) plus $0.06 per mile. Your actual rates will depend on your insurer and driver profile. Popular providers include Metromile, Milewise (Allstate), and SmartMiles (Nationwide).

Pay-per-mile insurance is typically best for drivers who log fewer than 8,000-10,000 miles per year — remote workers, retirees, city dwellers with multiple transport options, or households with a secondary vehicle that rarely gets used.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is my mileage tracked for pay-per-mile insurance?

Most pay-per-mile insurers use a small OBD-II device that plugs into your car's diagnostic port (usually under the dashboard) or a smartphone app. The device transmits your mileage data to the insurer. Some programs also track driving behavior like speed and braking.

Is there a daily or monthly mileage cap?

Some pay-per-mile insurers cap daily billable miles (e.g., at 150 or 250 miles), which can benefit occasional long-distance drivers. Check your specific insurer's cap policy before signing up.

Does pay-per-mile insurance provide the same coverage as standard insurance?

Yes. Pay-per-mile policies offer the same coverage options as standard auto insurance — liability, collision, comprehensive, uninsured motorist, etc. The only difference is how the premium is calculated.

What if I suddenly need to drive a lot more?

Your premium will increase proportionally with your miles. If you know you'll be driving significantly more for an extended period (e.g., a road trip or temporary commute change), compare whether standard insurance would be cheaper for that period.

Can I switch back to standard insurance from pay-per-mile?

Yes. You can switch at any time, typically at your renewal date or with notice. There are no long-term lock-in commitments with most pay-per-mile insurers.
