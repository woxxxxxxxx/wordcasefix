How This Calculator Works

This estimator uses a base rate of 2.5% of the car's purchase price per year, then applies adjustment factors:

  • Safety Rating: 5-star vehicles get a 10% discount; 3-star vehicles pay 10% more.
  • Car Type: Sports cars carry a 30% surcharge due to higher theft and accident rates; SUVs add 5%.
  • Driver Age: Drivers under 25 pay 80% more; drivers over 65 pay 20% more due to increased risk profiles.

The national average for full-coverage auto insurance is approximately $1,800-$2,200 per year. Your final quote depends on your specific insurer, state regulations, credit score, and driving record.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does a sports car cost more to insure?

Sports cars typically have higher repair costs, more powerful engines that increase accident severity, and higher theft rates. Insurers price these risks into the premium, often adding 25-40% above a comparable sedan.

How does car safety rating affect insurance rates?

Vehicles with higher safety ratings cause less severe injuries in crashes, reducing medical claim costs. A 5-star NHTSA rating can reduce your premium by 5-15% depending on the insurer.

Why do drivers under 25 pay so much more?

Statistically, drivers aged 16-24 have the highest accident rates. Inexperience combined with risk-taking behavior leads insurers to charge 50-100% more for young drivers compared to adults aged 25-65.

Does the car price directly affect my insurance premium?

Yes — more expensive cars cost more to repair or replace, directly increasing comprehensive and collision premiums. A $60,000 car will generally cost twice as much to insure as a $30,000 car, all else being equal.

How can I lower my new car insurance cost?

Choose a vehicle with good safety ratings, bundle your auto and home policies, maintain a clean driving record, increase your deductible, and shop multiple insurers. Telematics programs can also reduce premiums by 10-30% for safe drivers.
