How Home and Auto Bundling Works

When you purchase both your homeowners (or renters) and auto insurance from the same company, insurers typically reward you with a multi-policy discount — commonly called a bundle discount. This discount is applied to both policies and averages 10–25% depending on the insurer.

Beyond the savings, bundling simplifies your insurance management: one insurer, one renewal date (in many cases), and one customer service relationship. However, always compare bundled quotes against separate-carrier quotes — occasionally two different specialized insurers can beat a bundled price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which insurance companies offer the best bundle discounts?

Major carriers like State Farm, Allstate, Nationwide, and USAA are known for competitive bundle discounts, often 15–25%. The best deal depends on your location, home value, and driving profile. Always get multiple bundled quotes.

Does bundling always save money?

Usually, but not always. In some cases, a specialist insurer for home coverage combined with a separate auto insurer may be cheaper than any bundled option. Run the numbers both ways before committing.

Can renters insurance be bundled with auto?

Yes. Most insurers that offer bundle discounts include renters insurance as well as homeowners. Renters insurance is typically inexpensive ($15–$30/month), so the bundle discount on your auto policy alone often pays for it.

What happens to my discount if I cancel one policy?

If you cancel either policy, you lose the bundle discount on both. Your remaining policy will revert to its standard (non-bundled) rate at your next renewal or sooner, depending on your insurer's terms.

Can I bundle more than two policies?

Yes. Many insurers allow you to bundle auto, home, life, umbrella, and other policies. Each additional policy may increase your total discount. Ask your insurer about their multi-policy discount tiers.
