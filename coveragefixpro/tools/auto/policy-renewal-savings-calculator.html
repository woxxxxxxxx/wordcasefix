How This Calculator Works

This tool compares your current annual premium against a new quote, accounts for one-time switching costs, and projects your 3-year net savings:

  • Annual Savings = Current Premium − New Quote
  • 3-Year Net Savings = (Annual Savings × 3) − Switching Cost
  • If 3-year net savings > $0, switching is financially beneficial.

Switching costs may include cancellation fees (usually $25–$50), new policy fees, or losing a loyalty discount. Always confirm your current policy's cancellation terms before switching.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to switch car insurance?

The best time is at your policy renewal date to avoid cancellation fees. However, you can switch mid-policy — most insurers refund unused premium on a pro-rated basis. Start shopping 3–4 weeks before renewal for the best results.

Will switching insurers affect my coverage?

Not if you choose equivalent or better coverage. Always compare deductibles, limits, and included features side-by-side. A lower premium with significantly less coverage may cost more in the long run after a claim.

Do I lose loyalty discounts when I switch?

Yes — most long-term policyholder discounts are lost when switching. Factor this into your comparison. However, new customer discounts at the new insurer can offset this, and rates typically reset to market rates after several years anyway.

How often should I shop for car insurance?

Insurance experts recommend shopping every 1–2 years. Life changes like marriage, moving, buying a new car, or turning 25 often unlock lower rates. Even without changes, competitive pricing shifts constantly.

What if my new quote is higher?

Use the higher quote as leverage to negotiate with your current insurer. Call their retention department and mention competitor pricing — many will match or beat the offer to keep your business.
