Is No-Medical-Exam Life Insurance Worth the Extra Cost?

No-medical-exam life insurance (also called simplified issue or accelerated underwriting) lets you skip the blood draw, urinalysis, and physical examination required for traditional policies. The trade-off is higher premiums — typically 20–40% more than a fully underwritten policy. For healthy individuals, the convenience isn't worth the extra cost. However, for those with minor health conditions who might face higher ratings or even denial with a traditional exam, no-exam policies provide a practical and accessible coverage option. The coverage limits for no-exam policies are typically capped at $1–3 million depending on the insurer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is simplified issue life insurance?

Simplified issue policies ask a few health questions but don't require a medical exam. Approval is faster — sometimes same-day — but premiums are higher than fully underwritten policies.

What is accelerated underwriting?

Accelerated underwriting uses algorithms, prescription databases, and other data sources to approve applicants without a traditional exam. Healthy applicants may qualify for standard or preferred rates without an exam.

How much coverage can I get without an exam?

Most no-exam policies cap coverage at $500,000 to $3 million depending on the insurer and your age. For larger coverage amounts, a traditional medical exam is typically required.

Who benefits most from no-exam life insurance?

People with minor, controlled health conditions (e.g., managed hypertension or diabetes) who might face higher rates or lengthy underwriting with a traditional exam often benefit most from no-exam options.

Can I switch to a lower-cost policy later?

Yes — if your health remains good, you can apply for a fully underwritten policy later and potentially secure lower premiums. However, any new health conditions diagnosed between applications will affect your new rates.
