About Commercial Umbrella Insurance

Commercial umbrella insurance provides an extra layer of liability protection above and beyond the limits of your existing general liability, commercial auto, and employer's liability policies. When a covered claim exhausts your underlying policy limits, umbrella coverage kicks in. For example, if you have $1 million in GL coverage and face a $2.5 million judgment, your umbrella policy covers the remaining $1.5 million. Commercial umbrella is one of the most cost-effective ways to increase your overall liability protection — a $1 million umbrella policy typically costs just $750–$1,000 per year. High-risk businesses, those with significant assets, or those with large commercial contracts should consider $5–10 million in umbrella coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between commercial umbrella and excess liability?
Commercial umbrella insurance can broaden coverage beyond your underlying policies while also increasing limits. Excess liability strictly increases limits without broadening coverage. Umbrella policies typically offer more comprehensive protection.
How much umbrella coverage does a business need?
A common guideline is to carry umbrella coverage equal to at least your business net assets. High-risk industries, businesses with large contracts, and those with commercial fleets often need $5–10 million in umbrella limits.
What underlying policies does a commercial umbrella require?
Most umbrella policies require you to maintain minimum limits on your underlying policies — typically $1 million GL, $1 million commercial auto, and $500,000 employer's liability. The umbrella sits on top of these.
Does a commercial umbrella cover professional liability?
Standard commercial umbrella policies do not extend over professional liability (E&O) or D&O policies. You need separate excess professional liability coverage to increase those limits.
Is commercial umbrella insurance expensive?
No — it is one of the best values in business insurance. A $1 million umbrella typically costs $750–$1,500 per year for most businesses. Each additional million in coverage costs less than the first million.
