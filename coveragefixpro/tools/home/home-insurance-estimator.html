About This Estimator

Homeowners insurance premiums vary widely based on your home's value, age, location, and construction materials. States like Florida and Texas typically carry higher premiums due to hurricane and storm risk, while masonry construction often earns a discount. Newer homes built after 2010 may also qualify for lower rates thanks to modern building codes. Use this estimator as a starting point, then compare quotes from multiple licensed insurers to find the best rate for your specific property.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does homeowners insurance cover?

Standard homeowners insurance (HO-3) covers dwelling damage, personal property, liability, and additional living expenses if your home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered peril.

How is the homeowners insurance premium calculated?

Insurers consider home value, location, construction type, age, claims history, credit score, and selected coverage limits and deductibles when calculating your premium.

Why are Florida premiums so much higher?

Florida faces significant hurricane, wind, and flood risks, leading insurers to charge substantially higher premiums. Many major carriers have also exited the Florida market, reducing competition.

Does masonry construction lower my premium?

Yes. Brick, stone, and concrete homes are more fire-resistant than wood-frame homes, which typically results in a 5–15% discount on your homeowners insurance premium.

How often should I review my homeowners insurance?

Review your policy annually, after major renovations, or whenever you acquire high-value items. Inflation and rising construction costs can leave you underinsured if coverage isn't updated regularly.
