Frequently Asked Questions

What is the formula for Celsius to Fahrenheit?
°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32. For example, 100 °C = (100 × 1.8) + 32 = 212 °F (water's boiling point at sea level).
At what temperature are Celsius and Fahrenheit equal?
At −40°. That is the single point where both scales read the same number: −40 °C = −40 °F.
Does this tool work offline?
